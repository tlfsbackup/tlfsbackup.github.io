      Obvious information:

       

      1. The Song is Waterfront performed by Simple Minds
      2. Is has been performed live in Edinburgh in the Usher Hall in April 2015

       

      Inside GTAF information:

       

      1. The video has been featured in the forums first on February 12th 2020 in this thread
      2. Together with the Video, P wrote :

       

      Aye Jimbo

      Remember that time

      Ye moved intae

      Yon big flat across thi road

       

      Literally ye

      Wanted tae

      Move on up

      Tae the Dumbiebdykes

      Tae get oota thi rain 

       

      First quick search resulted in the following GTA-related information:

       

      1. Simple Minds were featured in the GTA V soundtrack with the song "All the Things She Said" on Los Santos Rock Radio
      2. Waterfront connects to the Waterfront Promenade in the GTA titles:The Waterfront Promenade is a Liberty City promenade on the east of the borough of Portland in Grand Theft Auto III, Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories, and Grand Theft Auto Advance.

      3. Dumbiedykes is a residential area in the centre of Edinburgh.

        It is bounded in the north by Holyrood Road, the west by the Pleasance and St Leonard's Street and the east by Holyrood Park. If you visit 108 Holyrood Rd, you'll be standing in front of the door of the Rockstar North headquarters.

       

      Cross references:

       

      The citation is probably from the Dead Souls Novel written by Ian Rankin. Main location is Edinburgh and it featured Inspector REBUS. One of the charakters is Jim Stevens, who goes by the nickname Jimbo.

       

      Spiritual Investigator Name:

       

      1. Ramsay points towards the scottish partiot Alexander Ramsay of Dalhousie. Dalhousie Castle is only 8 miles away from Edinburgh.
      2. There is a minor character by the name Graham Ramsay featured in GTA V.

       

      Upcoming questions:

       

      1. Why is Edinburgh emphasized so strongly in almost all the viddles?
      2. Why is there a strong connection to all the scottish investigators? Is it just to support the "scottish theme" of everything or does it have a deeper meaning? (I have checked Ian Rankin but found no real connection to Rockstar Games or GTA in his Twitter and Facebook. The only thing is an introduction he wrote to James Crumleys book "The Last Good Kiss". But James Crumley is already dead and the book was written in 1978. The description of the book includes "grand theft auto" but it's meant litterally as the crime, not the game)
      3. Why is Usher Hall so relevant? It refers to the thread in these forums. But back then (2018) it probably was only about RDR2. Or was it? The thread contains several connections to the 80s like references to a Delorean. This question generates some follow-up questions:
      • If it was not just a press event for RDR2 but also GTA VI, wouldn't the mods like @Kirsty or @Spider-Vice know about it? If the reference "follow the white bag" is correct, at least one of them was attending that event.
      • If it was divided into two parts, one for press and one for Rockstar-employees, would it make sense to announce GTA VI being Vice that late? I mean it was 2018. I don't know much about game developement but at this point, the works for GTA VI should have been up and running for at least a few years. So the employees would have know already...
      • Maybe there was another event planned at Usher Hall for the end of March 2020? According to Edinburgh News it had to shut down due to Corona starting on the 17th of March 2020.

      It could be referencing the rebirth of a City, we have seen in older GTA-titles.

      Ian Rankin called the rebuilt tenement area "Greenfield" in his novel Dead Souls (1999)

       

      --

       

      Greenfield or Greenfields may refer to:

       

      Engineering
      Greenfield land, a piece of undeveloped land (the opposite of brownfield land)


      One of several related terms:
      Greenfield project, a project which lacks any constraints imposed by prior work
      Greenfield agreement, an employment agreement for a new organisation
      Greenfield investment, the investment in a structure in an area where no previous facilities exist
      Greenfield status, a term used after a decommissioned site is restored to its original condition prior to any development

       

      --

       

      William Greenfield was a Scottish minister, professor of rhetoric and belles lettres, literary critic, reviewer, and author whose clerical career ended in scandal, resulting in him being excommunicated from the Church of Scotland. A friend and correspondent of Robert Burns and a beneficiary of Walter Scott. Robert Burns and Walter Scott are both great poets of Scotland.
