      CASE #008

       

      Welcome brothers and sisters. Please take a moment and remember to be kind to one another. No thought or theory is to be ridiculed or put down. We are all equals here and thus our thoughts are all equally weighted. I feel blessed to be among you, working diligently towards our common goal. Let's begin:

       

      Original Post [5th February, 2020] - The Preacher's fourth post in the infamous 'Will There Be Realistic Eastenders in GTAVI?'


      'The Wizard of Oz' - a 1939 film starring Judy Garland as Dorothy, a young girl who gets taken by a tornado and thrown from her hometown in Kansas and taken to the wonderful world of Oz.

       

      "We're not in Kansas anymore" has become a phrase commonly used when one steps outside of the comforts of their regular life. Here is an interesting post about the usage of the idiom. 

      As a small aside, I would be remiss to not bring this up: The Heartlands, in the State of New Hanover in Rockstar Games' Red Dead Redemption II, are partly based on great plain regions such as Oklahoma, Nebraska, and Kansas. Is it possible that we will - quite literally - no longer be in Kansas anymore? Are we moving further from realistic tumbleweed?

       

      I look forward to reading your further discoveries.
