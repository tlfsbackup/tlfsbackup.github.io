      CURRENT LEADS:

      1. THE BIBLE: in the album's Wikipedia page we can find a note by the author of the song, who explains his inspiration behind it:

      Quote

      «Ever since I can remember hearing the Lazarus story, when I was a kid, you know, back in church, I was disturbed and worried by it. Traumatised, actually. We are all, of course, in awe of the greatest of Christ's miracles—raising a man from the dead—but I couldn't help but wonder how Lazarus felt about it. As a child it gave me the creeps, to be honest. I've taken Lazarus and stuck him in New York City, in order to give the song, a hip, contemporary feel.»

      Could the meaning of the riddle have anything to do with the biblical event known as the Raising of Lazarus or his character altogether? How does that tie in with the lyrics of the song?

      There are 2 mentions in the bible of Lazarus:

       

      The story we all know – Jesus Raising Lazarus from the dead

      Jesus loved Lazarus and his sisters and that when Lazarus died of illness, Jesus wept and was “greatly disturbed.” Although Lazarus had been entombed for four days by the time Jesus arrived at Bethany, he was raised by Jesus from the dead and emerged from the tomb wearing his burial cloths. This miracle, witnessed by many Jews who had come to grieve with the family, inspired many to believe in Jesus as the Christ.

       

      The second instance of Lazarus is in Luke 16:19-31 – The Rich Man and Lazarus. This story essentially talks about the rich man having a lavish life of privilege, while Lazarus spends his life as a beggar. Upon their deaths, Lazarus ends up in Heaven and the rich man ends up in hell. Reminiscent of a quote from Jesus: The last shall be first and the first last.

       

      2. THE BAND – Nick Cave & the Bad Seeds

      The band is Australian and was formed in 1983, fitting our 1980’s theme.

      Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds have been considered “one of the most original and celebrated bands of the post-punk and alternative rock eras in the ‘80s and onward”.

       

      3. POSSIBLE GTA TIE-INS

      Lyrics:  

      He came from New York City, but he couldn’t take the place. Then he went to San Francisco

      After another chorus, the lyrics are: New York City, he had to get out of there
      And San Francisco, well, I don't know
      And then to LA, where he spent about a day
      He thought even the pale sky-stars were smart enough to keep well away from LA

      Interestingly enough, this lyric follows the GTA release timeline…sort of. GTA 1 takes place in Liberty City (New York City), San Andreas (San Francisco) and eventually Vice City (not mentioned in the lyrics)

      Alternatively, if you begin in the GTA 3D era, GTA 3 takes place in Liberty City (New York City) THEN we travel to San Andreas (San Francisco) and then we eventually make it to Los Santos (L.A)

      He ended up, like so many of them do, back in the streets of New York City
      In a soup queue
      A dope fiend
      A slave
      Then prison
      Then the mad house
      Then the grave
      Oh poor Larry

      Heading back to Liberty City? Maybe, if the multiple locations theory is accurate. Maybe the protag will serve some time in prison, contend with an addiction and eventually die? ****RDR2 SPOILER*** We already know R* had no problem killing protagonists.

      POSSIBLE RDR2 LEADS

      The character Benjamin Lazarus is a magician that performs escape style and death defying feats like Houdini, including escaping from a straight jacket while hanging, escaping a trunk and catching a bullet. Lazarus is essentially the R* representation of Houdini.

      Thoughts:

      Why Lazarus?

      This video was posted on February 6th – long before there were talks of banning the preacher and talks about a possible resurrection that would later take place in April. Assuming P didn’t predict this or plan the whole thing in advance, we should consider the meaning of this video prior to the last fish supper.

      Lazarus could mean a resurrection of the GTA franchise.

      Consider the position of Lazarus in the song – He’s seems low on the totem pole – a worker bee. He understands something is happening upstairs, but he doesn’t know what it is. The people upstairs are ordering him to dig himself back in to his hole – a metaphor for getting himself in to an awkward or restrictive situation. We know that Jason Schreier wrote an article about the touch working conditions at R*. Perhaps this is a hint that the higher-ups are ramping up production.
