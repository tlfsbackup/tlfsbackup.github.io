      LuckyMe is a record label based in the UK founded in July 7, 2007, part of whose branding features our favorite eye. One of the founders, Hudson Mohawk, has a song on GTA V's FlyLo FM "100hm". Fairly apt. 100 hectometers = 32000 feet. Obviously we can't reach those exact heights in GTA V, but it's all relative. Simply put, Dom, Franklin, and Jeff take to high pressure altitudes using aircraft like Jets to perform their daredevil-ish stunts.

       

      On LuckyMe's vimeo description for the Cool World, they describe the band American Men as:

      Quote

      a four-piece synthetic rock band formed of Claude Speed, Alilloyd, Scottt Chevrolet, Steven Shade - all members of Dananananakroyd or other obscure and fantastic Glasgow Rock bands.

       

      Their debut release, Cool World, features 5 shimmering tracks with additional gatefold disc of remixes by Hudson Mohawke, Falty DL, Ikonika & Machine Drum. Available now at thisisluckyme.com/html/1music/store.html

       

      Pick up free mixtapes from the band at thisisluckyme.com

       

      American Men's Cool World EP was released on July 6, 2010, and includes a track called "Claude Speed" after one of their members, as well as of course the protagonist of GTA2. There's not a whole lot I can find about American Men or this particular Claude Speed, whether it is intentional a reference to GTA or not.

       

      In the description of "Claude Speed":

      Quote

      "Buffed by adulation from all corners of the industry... American Men deliver their first five tracks of Miami-lazer-rock for LuckyMe. The Scottish group comprise three members of the short lived Russia outfit and Dananananaykroyd, playing a canny fusion of complex Math Rock and arcade game melodies... Their sound is an intense blast of nostalgia delivered with incredibly sharp musicianship and an acutely aware avant-rock knowledge that's gained them fans from Mary Anne Hobbs to Mark Pritchard and Radio 1's Huw Stephens. The remixes range from Ikonika and Optimum's 8-bit Funky ride to Falty DL's febrile beat collage and a boldly epic recomposition from Hud Mo. Recommended." - Boomkat Review
